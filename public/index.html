About

I'm a final year BSc Software Engineering student who builds things to understand them. My background is in software engineering and mathematics, and that combination has never felt coincidental. Maths gave me a way of thinking about problems that goes deeper than tutorials and frameworks. It's what eventually pulled me toward AI and machine learning, not as a buzzword but as an engineering discipline I wanted to actually understand.

Specifically, I'm interested in natural language processing and language models. How they learn structure from text, how retrieval augmented generation improves accuracy on real documents, how to design pipelines that hold up outside of demos. That's where I'm putting my learning right now, on top of the software engineering foundation I already have.

As a developer, I build full systems: backends, databases, APIs, cloud infrastructure. Everything in the projects section below is something I designed and built from scratch. No tutorials followed step by step. I pick a problem, figure out what it needs, and build it until it works. The way I learn is simple: if something is needed, I go learn it. That is how Docker, Terraform, RAG pipelines and vector databases ended up in my toolkit. I pick things up fast because I genuinely want to understand them, not just use them. The curiosity does not stop.

Oh, one more thing. I am a New York Knicks fan. Not a casual one. The kind that had 2026 circled before the season started and was insufferable about it the entire time. First chip since 1973. Brunson Finals MVP. First Cup and title double in league history. I built an entire app about it because apparently that is a normal thing to do when your team wins. If you want to see what happens when software engineering meets completely unhinged fandom, that is on you.
